# Cascade Loop - 3-Day Guided Trek

**Vendor:** Summitline Outfitters Guiding Services
**Document type:** Trail guide
**Publication date:** 2026-03-10
**Route difficulty:** Moderate to strenuous
**Listed fee (per guest):** 895.00 USD

## Summary

A three-day, two-night guided loop through the North Cascades, connecting
the Ridge Camp, Aster Basin, and Glacier Peak lookouts. Roughly 28 miles of
trail with 6,400 ft of elevation gain. Led by an AMGA-certified mountain
guide and rated moderate to strenuous. The trip fee of **895.00 USD per
guest** covers permits, group gear, and guiding services.

## Itinerary

### Day 1 - Trailhead to Ridge Camp (8.2 mi, +2,800 ft)

Early start from the North Fork trailhead. Steady climb through old-growth
western hemlock, then a sustained switchback ascent to Ridge Camp at 5,400
ft. Sunset views of the Picket Range.

### Day 2 - Ridge Camp to Aster Basin (11.5 mi, +2,100 ft, -1,700 ft)

Long traverse across the Ridge divide, one permanent snowfield crossing
(ice axe required June through mid-July), then a gentle descent into Aster
Basin, a flower meadow at 4,900 ft well-protected from afternoon winds.

### Day 3 - Aster Basin to Glacier Peak trailhead (8.3 mi, +1,500 ft, -4,200 ft)

Final climb to the Glacier Peak fire lookout (6,150 ft, panoramic), then a
long descent through alpine larch and talus back to the trailhead by early
afternoon.

## Required gear

- Summit Ascent 3P tent (group-issued)
- Glacier 15F sleeping bag, rated to -9 C (guest-supplied or rental)
- Crestline 28L daypack or equivalent
- Ridgeline waterproof hardshell jacket
- Ice axe and microspikes (season-dependent)

## Safety notes

Weather in the North Cascades changes fast. This route crosses one permanent
snowfield and two seasonal creek fords. Guests must sign the standard
liability waiver. Minimum age 16; guests under 18 require a parent or
guardian on-trip.
