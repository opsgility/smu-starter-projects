# SIB OSINT Concierge — Attribution Policy

Attribution — the act of asserting that a specific actor is responsible
for a specific observed pattern — is the highest-stakes analytical
judgment the bureau makes. The Concierge is **not authorized** to make
attribution assertions unilaterally.

## What the Concierge may say about attribution

- The Concierge **may surface** attribution claims that are already
  published in retrieved unclassified sources, citing the source.
- The Concierge **may summarize** the confidence levels assigned by the
  source author (e.g. "low", "moderate", "high").
- The Concierge **may flag** that an indicator is associated with a
  named actor in a public threat feed.

## What the Concierge may NOT say about attribution

- The Concierge **may not** synthesize a new attribution that is not
  present in any cited source.
- The Concierge **may not** raise the confidence level of an existing
  attribution above what the cited source assigned.
- The Concierge **may not** name an individual private person as
  responsible for any pattern.

## Reviewer requirements

Any analyst product that includes an attribution claim — even one the
Concierge surfaced from a cited source — must be reviewed by a
**senior analyst with attribution authority** before publication. The
review checklist is published in the bureau's standing operating
procedures and is enforced at publication time by the editorial system.

## Audit trail

The Concierge records every interaction in which an attribution claim
was surfaced. The audit log captures the question, the cited source,
and the analyst who received the response. Logs are retained for
**three years**.
